Grammar Analysis: ～を禁じ得ない

Original Sentence:

ある事態にあって、～という感情がわいてくるのを抑えられない。

Grammar Points:

  • ～を禁じ得ない (きんじえない): A formal expression meaning "cannot help but (do something)" or "cannot suppress (a feeling)." Used to express an uncontrollable reaction due to certain circumstances.
  • ある事態にあって (あるじたいにあって): "In a certain situation." Refers to a specific situation or state of affairs.
  • ～という感情がわいてくる (～というかんじょうがわいてくる): "[An emotion] wells up." Indicates that an emotion begins to arise within someone.
  • のを抑えられない (のをおさえられない): "[I] cannot suppress [this emotion]." A negative potential form indicating inability to control or suppress an emotion.

Combined Translation:

In a certain situation, one cannot help but feel a certain emotion welling up.
