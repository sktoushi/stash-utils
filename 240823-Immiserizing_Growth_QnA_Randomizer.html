Immiserizing Growth (Section 7.5)

Immiserizing growth refers to a situation where economic growth, particularly through technical progress or expansion of production, paradoxically leads to a decline in a nation's overall welfare. This occurs despite increases in production and exports because of adverse changes in the terms of trade.

Figure 7.6 Explanation

The figure shows the production frontier of a nation (Nation 1) before and after neutral technical progress. This technical progress increases productivity in the production of commodity X.

  • Production Frontier Shift: The production frontier shifts outward from \( PB = 1 \) to \( PC = rac15 \), indicating that Nation 1 can now produce more of commodity X.
  • Terms of Trade Deterioration: Despite this increase in productivity, the terms of trade for Nation 1 deteriorate drastically, moving from point B (before growth) to point C (after growth).
  • Welfare Effect: Nation 1’s welfare is negatively affected. Although increased production would typically raise welfare, the worsening terms of trade place the nation on a lower indifference curve, indicating lower welfare.

Why Immiserizing Growth Occurs

  • Nation 1’s Large Scale: Significant increases in exports by a large country can worsen its terms of trade due to global price adjustments.
  • Elasticity of Demand: Low elasticity of demand for Nation 1's goods means that increased exports don’t lead to proportionately higher revenues, worsening terms of trade.
  • Dependence on Trade: High trade dependence can amplify the negative welfare impact when terms of trade deteriorate.
