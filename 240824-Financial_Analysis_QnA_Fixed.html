Financial Analysis of Ernie Bishop Company (2012-2013)

Step 1: Calculate the Liquidity Ratios

Liquidity ratios generally include the current ratio and the quick ratio.

  • Current Ratio (2013): 4.07
  • Current Ratio (2012): 4.08
  • Quick Ratio (2013): 2.71
  • Quick Ratio (2012): 2.60

Step 2: Calculate the Profitability Ratios

Profitability ratios generally include the gross profit margin and the net profit margin.

  • Gross Profit Margin (2013): 28.8%
  • Gross Profit Margin (2012): 27.9%
  • Net Profit Margin (2013): 4.95%
  • Net Profit Margin (2012): 5.26%

Step 3: Analysis of Changes

  • Liquidity: The current ratio remained stable with a slight decrease from 4.08 in 2012 to 4.07 in 2013. The quick ratio improved from 2.60 in 2012 to 2.71 in 2013, suggesting a marginally better position to meet short-term obligations without relying on inventory.
  • Profitability: The gross profit margin improved slightly from 27.9% in 2012 to 28.8% in 2013. However, the net profit margin decreased from 5.26% in 2012 to 4.95% in 2013, indicating that the overall profitability after all expenses have been deducted from revenue slightly declined.

Conclusion

Liquidity: The liquidity of Ernie Bishop Company is stable with a slight improvement in the quick ratio, suggesting a marginally better position to meet short-term obligations without relying on inventory.

Profitability: Although the gross profit margin improved, the net profit margin slightly decreased, which could be a concern if this trend continues. The decrease in net profit margin suggests that operating expenses have increased more than gross profit, possibly due to higher operating costs or other non-operating expenses.

This mixed performance indicates that while the company is doing slightly better in terms of liquidity and gross profitability, the increase in operating expenses needs to be managed to avoid further erosion in net profitability.
